# CS-330
1. How do I approach designing software?
I approach designing software by first understanding the problem and then planning out how everything should be structured before I start coding. This project helped me get better at breaking larger problems into smaller pieces and thinking about how those pieces connect, especially when it comes to choosing the right data structures and organizing program flow. I followed a step-by-step design process where I reviewed requirements, mapped out how data would move through the program, and then built it in stages. One of the biggest takeaways is how valuable that upfront planning is—it makes the actual coding smoother and helps avoid bigger issues later. These same design tactics can easily carry over into future projects by helping me build more efficient, scalable, and organized solutions from the start.

2. How do I approach developing programs?
When developing programs, I focus on building things in small, manageable steps instead of trying to do everything at once. During the 3D scene project, I relied a lot on incremental development—adding one object, texture, or lighting change at a time and testing it immediately. Iteration played a huge role because I was constantly adjusting and refining based on how the scene looked and performed. Over the course of the milestones, my approach shifted from more trial-and-error to being more structured and intentional, which made debugging easier and helped me stay on track. By the end of the project, I was much more confident in building, testing, and refining code in a way that leads to a solid final product.

3. How can computer science help me in reaching my goals?
Computer science gives me practical problem-solving skills that I can apply in both technical and real-world situations. Working with computational graphics and visualizations helped me understand how programming, math, and visual design all come together, which adds another layer to my skill set. From an educational standpoint, this prepares me for more advanced topics like simulations, UI design, and interactive systems. Professionally, these skills can apply to areas like development, visualization tools, or even industries that rely on modeling and design. Overall, it’s helping me build a strong foundation that I can use to grow in the tech field and take on more complex projects in the future.
